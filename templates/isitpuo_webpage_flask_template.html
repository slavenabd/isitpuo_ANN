Predicting posterior urethral obstruction in boys with lower urinary tract symptoms using deep artifitial neural network

Introduction:

Congenital posterior urethral obstruction (POO) is congenital obstruction of urethra in boys which manifests as posterior urethral valve (PUV) in infancy. However, POO can also manifest in preshool and school-age boys when it presents with weak or intermittent stream, straining, hesistancy, but also with urgency and extrodinary daytime frequency. These symptoms usually occur without hydronephrosis, vesicourethral reflux, or infection, which are charateristic for PUV of infancy.

Aim of this study was to report frequency of POO in preschool and school age boys with LUTS in outpatient clinic, investigate clinical and diagnostical variables which are significantlly correlated with the finding of POO on cystoscopy, and to develop classification deep neural network for the prediction of POO in boys with LUTS.
